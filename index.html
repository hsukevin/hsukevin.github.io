Projects

Kronos Project Manager

I was chosen by my team to be the project manager for the CSE 110, Software Engineering. I was expected to appoint the rest of the team their positions, make executive decisions, and keep my team on track to success. The goal of our app is to simplify the process of making to-do lists and schedules for students and allowing teachers to broadcast class schedules efficiently. For the app, we needed to set up both a user database and an events database for users to follow. On top of that, our app needed all core features of a calendar app. I was voted as the top contributing member in the first round of peer reviews.






IEEE Micromouse Lead

One of ten selected leads, I commanded a team of 7 other fellow IEEE Micromouse members. Together, our goal was to build an autonomous robot that would be able to traverse a maze and solve for the shortest path to the center. From doing research, to soldering wires, we built our mouse piece by piece. Afterwards, we wrote the code and tried to program the mouse to solve the maze. Unfortunately, one of our encoders malfunctioned near the date of the competition and we were unable to participate.

Project Euler

Project Euler was a pastime that I enjoyed solving. It is an archive of mind boggling problems that were based around coding and math. I competed with my friends to see who ran the fastest algorithm or solved the problem quickest. After a few problems however, we felt like these brain teasers were getting dull and wanted to pursue building something more meaningful. Maybe I will return to doing more problems, but for now, I am leaving Project Euler to rest and pursue learning different skill sets.
