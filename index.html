Projects

CSE 110 - Team Kronos Project Manager

For this software industry oriented course, I was chosen by my team to be the project manager. I was expected to appoint the rest of the team their positions, make executive decisions, and keep my team on track to success. The goal of our app is to simplify the process of making to-do lists and schedules for students and allowing teachers to broadcast class schedules efficiently. For the app, we needed to set up both a user database and an events database for users to follow. On top of that, our app needed all core features of a calendar app. I was voted as the top contributing member in the first round of peer reviews, and am ecstatic about the minimalistic app we are planning to ship.

IEEE - Micromouse Lead 2014-2015

As one of the ten selected leads, I commanded a team of 7 other fellow IEEE Micromouse student members. Together, our goal was to build an autonomous robot from scratch that would be able to travers a maze and solve for the shortest path to the center. We started out by researching the project and trying not to reinvent the wheel. We then proceeded to buy the parts and assemble parts. Along the process, we had to solder wires and plan out our mouse, piece by piece. Afterwards, we continued to program our mouse and code it to solve the maze. My team is due to compete with the other teams' mouses in May 2015.
