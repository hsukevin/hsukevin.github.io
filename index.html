IEEE Micromouse Lead

As one of ten selected leads, I commanded a team of 7 other fellow IEEE Micromouse members. Together, our goal was to build an autonomous robot that would be able to traverse a maze and compute the shortest path to the center. From doing research, buying parts online, soldering wires, we built our mouse piece by piece together as a team. It was a very involved process learning about the sensors, encoders, motors, battery, and Teensy development board that runs an ARM processor. After assembling the mouse, we wrote the code and tried to program the mouse to solve the maze. Unfortunately, one of our encoders malfunctioned near the date of the competition and we were unable to participate. I would love to participate in the Micromouse competition again, but it is far too time consuming. I realized that it is, indeed, rather a fascinating feat to finish but I would rather spend my time coding up my next application. Nonetheless, it was a worthwhile experience in that I learned that I would rather focus solely on software rather than mechanical specs. It helped in finding myself, which is a very important part of life.

Project Euler

Project Euler was a pastime that I enjoyed spending time on. It is an archive of mind boggling problems that were based around coding and math. I competed with my friends to see who ran the fastest algorithm or solved the problem quickest. Some of the problems were quite tricky and we really had to think on how to optimize on better big O runtime because it wouldn't process the answers in acceptable time. Other problems were really annoying to solve because of the input parameters. However, I felt like these brain teasers were getting dull and wanted to pursue building something more meaningful. Rather than tackling preexisting math problems already solved by others, I want to solve a problem that I have with my own unique solution. Maybe I will return to doing more problems, but for now, I am leaving Project Euler to pursue learning different skill sets to further my skillset.
